# Review: Elder Rasband & President Oaks — October Conference Talks

## Elder Ronald A. Rasband

---

### 1. "The Family Proclamation — Words from God" (October 2025)

**Session**: Saturday Afternoon
**Link**: [churchofjesuschrist.org](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2025/10/21rasband?lang=eng)

#### Context

October 2025 marked the 30th anniversary of "The Family: A Proclamation to the World." Elder Rasband used this milestone to reaffirm the proclamation's divine origin and continuing relevance. He shared that in 1994, a year before it was presented, the Quorum of the Twelve discussed how society and governments were pulling away from God's laws for family, marriage, and gender. The apostles who prepared it were "seers called of God."

#### Key Themes

- **Divine origin**: "The proclamation has a divine origin, so we must treat it with the reverence deserving words from God." It is doctrine, as President Hinckley stated in 1995.
- **Divine identity**: Every person is "a beloved spirit son or daughter of heavenly parents... [with] a divine nature and destiny."
- **Equal partnership**: "Fathers and mothers are obligated to help one another as equal partners." He emphasized that *preside* does not mean dominate and *nurture* does not mean secondary. He shared how Sister Rasband once corrected him for making a decision without her, shaping their commitment to equality.
- **Compassion for those who struggle**: "Stay with us. You live in challenging times when the adversary seeks to make you his. Do not be drawn off. And if you are, come back." Christ Himself offers strength and belonging.
- **Family happiness**: "Happiness in family life is most likely to be achieved when founded upon the teachings of Jesus Christ."

#### Key Quotes

> "The principles are not out of step, but perfectly in step with the ways of the Lord and His covenant path."

> "You have family in your Savior, Jesus Christ, who loves you. He came to earth to atone for our sins and bear the burden of our mistakes and our very bad days."

> "Our Father in Heaven has provided the proclamation on the family to help guide us home to Him."

#### Scriptures Referenced
- The Family: A Proclamation to the World (1995)

### 2. "Behold I Am the Light Which Ye Shall Hold Up" (October 2024)

**Session**: Sunday Afternoon
**Link**: [churchofjesuschrist.org](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2024/10/53rasband?lang=eng)

#### Context

Elder Rasband drew on the Savior's words to the Nephites (3 Nephi 18:24) — "Hold up your light that it may shine unto the world. Behold I am the light which ye shall hold up" — to teach about sustaining prophets and holding fast to covenants.

#### Key Themes

- **Apostolic witness**: Jesus Christ is the Son of God, our Redeemer. By His Atonement, we can return to the Father and be with our families for eternity. "The Savior is not absent from our mortal journeys."
- **Holding up the light**: We hold up the Lord's light through covenant faithfulness and by supporting the living prophet.
- **Active sustaining**: Quoting President Nelson: "Our sustaining of prophets is a personal commitment that we will do our utmost to uphold their prophetic priorities." This is not passive — we defend, follow, teach, and pray.
- **Temples as places of peace**: At the Manti Utah Temple rededication, President Nelson petitioned that the temple would "hold up" all who entered. Elder Rasband emphasized that temples counter the fear, darkness, and contention of the world.
- **Discerning truth from counterfeits**: We need to distinguish God's truths from Satan's deceptions through constant prayer.

#### Key Quotes

> "We hold up the Lord's light when we hold fast to our covenants and when we support our living prophet as he speaks the words of God."

> "To hold up the Prophet is a sacred work."

> "Time and again, with His pure love and mercy, He sustains us as we face the drama of life."

#### Scriptures Referenced
- 3 Nephi 18:24 ("Behold I am the light which ye shall hold up")
- 2 Nephi 4:20-21 ("My God hath been my support")
- Mosiah 2:11 (King Benjamin on service despite infirmities)

## President Dallin H. Oaks

---

### 3. "Following Christ" (October 2024)

**Session**: Saturday Morning
**Link**: [churchofjesuschrist.org](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2024/10/18oaks?lang=eng)

#### Context

As first counselor in the First Presidency, President Oaks spoke about what it means to truly follow Christ — not as a casual practice but as a continuous, life-defining commitment. He focused particularly on peacemaking over contention.

#### Key Themes

- **Continuous commitment**: "Following Christ is not a casual or occasional practice. It is a continuous commitment and way of life that should guide us at all times and in all places."
- **Universal invitation**: Christ invites all — "black and white, bond and free, male and female; ... and all are alike unto God" (2 Nephi 26:33).
- **Peacemaking over contention**: Quoting President Nelson: "Contention is a choice. Peacemaking is a choice. You have your agency to choose contention or reconciliation. I urge you to choose to be a peacemaker, now and always."
- **The Golden Rule**: Treat others as you would want to be treated — a foundational principle of discipleship.
- **Covenants as kite strings**: President Oaks compared covenants to the string that holds a kite aloft. Honoring covenants causes us to soar to celestial heights, not restricts us.
- **Letting God prevail**: Prioritize God's influence, commandments, and covenants above all other influences.

#### Key Quotes

> "Let us follow Him by forgoing contention. As we pursue our preferred policies in public actions, let us qualify for His blessings by using the language and methods of peacemakers."

> "In our families and other personal relationships, let us avoid what is harsh and hateful. Let us seek to be holy, like our Savior."

#### Scriptures Referenced
- 2 Nephi 26:33 ("all are alike unto God")
- Matthew 5:44 / 3 Nephi 12:44 ("love your enemies")
- The Great Commandments (Matthew 22:36-40)

### 4. "The Family-Centered Gospel of Jesus Christ" (October 2025)

**Session**: Sunday Afternoon (Closing Talk)
**Link**: [churchofjesuschrist.org](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2025/10/58oaks?lang=eng)

#### Context

President Oaks closed the October 2025 general conference (just days before being set apart as the 18th President of the Church on October 14, 2025, following President Nelson's passing). His address affirmed that the gospel plan centers on the family and addressed contemporary concerns about declining marriage and birthrates.

#### Key Themes

- **Family is central to God's plan**: "The gospel plan was first taught to us in the council of an eternal family, it is implemented through our mortal families, and its intended destiny is to exalt the children of God in eternal families."
- **Temple ordinances enable eternal families**: Temples are essential to the doctrine — ordinances received there enable members to return as eternal families.
- **Declining marriage and birthrates**: The U.S. has suffered deteriorating marriage and childbearing rates for nearly a century. Church members' rates, while more positive, have also declined. He stressed it is vital not to lose understanding of the purpose of marriage and the value of children.
- **Practical family strengthening**:
  - Pray together, kneeling night and morning
  - Worship together in Church services
  - Share family stories, traditions, and sacred experiences
  - Do meaningful activities together (gardens, camping, sports, reunions)
  - Teach children basic life skills
  - **Turn off technology**: "Many parents will find that they can turn their family on if they all turn their technologies off. And parents remember, what those children really want for dinner is time with you."
- **Non-traditional families**: Shared how his grandfather said "I will be your father" after President Oaks' father died — a powerful example of filling in the gaps.
- **Love for those who struggle**: Family members who don't embrace gospel values need love and patience. The perfection we seek is not limited to mortality — repentance and growth continue in the spirit world.
- **Christlike service**: "Following Christ and giving ourselves in service to one another is the best remedy for the selfishness and individualism that now seem to be so common."

#### Key Quotes

> "The gospel plan was first taught to us in the council of an eternal family, it is implemented through our mortal families, and its intended destiny is to exalt the children of God in eternal families."

> "Many parents will find that they can turn their family on if they all turn their technologies off."

> "I will be your father." — President Oaks' grandfather, after his father died

#### Scriptures Referenced
- Doctrine and Covenants 131-132 (temple sealing, eternal marriage)
- The Family: A Proclamation to the World

## Common Themes Across All Four Talks

### 1. The Family as God's Central Unit
Both speakers returned repeatedly to the family. Elder Rasband defended the Family Proclamation as doctrine with divine origin. President Oaks framed the entire gospel plan in family terms — taught in an eternal family, implemented through mortal families, destined for eternal families.

### 2. Covenant Faithfulness
Elder Rasband taught that holding up the light means holding fast to covenants. President Oaks compared covenants to a kite string that enables flight. Both see covenants not as restrictions but as enabling structures.

### 3. Compassion Without Compromise
Both speakers held firm on doctrine while extending deep compassion. Elder Rasband: "Stay with us... come back." President Oaks: growth and repentance continue beyond mortality, and those who struggle need love and patience, not judgment.

### 4. Active Discipleship
Following Christ is not passive. Elder Rasband called sustaining the prophet "a sacred work" — we defend, follow, teach, and pray. President Oaks said following Christ is "a continuous commitment and way of life," not casual or occasional.

### 5. Peacemaking and Unity
President Oaks emphasized choosing peacemaking over contention and using "the language and methods of peacemakers." Elder Rasband's emphasis on equal partnership in marriage reflects the same principle at the family level.

### 6. Christ as the Answer
Both point to Jesus Christ as the ultimate source of help. Elder Rasband: "You have family in your Savior, Jesus Christ." President Oaks: Christ is "our ultimate role model" and service to others is the remedy for selfishness.

## Parallels

### Programming
The covenant-as-kite-string metaphor from President Oaks maps to software constraints. In programming, constraints (type systems, interfaces, linting rules) feel restrictive but actually enable you to build more ambitious, reliable systems. A strongly-typed codebase "soars" because the constraints catch errors before they compound. Covenants function similarly — they're not limits on freedom but guardrails that enable higher spiritual achievement.

### Tolkien
Elder Rasband's plea — "Stay with us... come back" — echoes the mercy shown throughout Tolkien. Gandalf's insistence on mercy toward Gollum, Frodo's refusal to kill Saruman, Aragorn's welcome of the Oathbreakers who had once betrayed their covenant. The gospel pattern is the same: the door remains open for those who have wandered, and returning is always honored.

### Literature
President Oaks' grandfather saying "I will be your father" carries the weight of a covenant moment in literature — it echoes adoption narratives like Jean Valjean taking in Cosette in *Les Miserables*, where a single person's commitment to fill a gap reshapes a life entirely.

## Questions to Ponder

1. What does it look like to "actively sustain" the prophet in daily life, beyond raising my hand in conference?
2. Elder Rasband emphasized that "preside" does not mean "dominate." How do I ensure equal partnership in my closest relationships?
3. President Oaks said "what those children really want for dinner is time with you." Where am I letting technology substitute for genuine presence?
4. Both speakers extend compassion to those who struggle while affirming doctrine. How do I hold both of those at the same time in real conversations?
5. President Oaks taught that repentance and growth continue beyond mortality. How does that expand my understanding of God's patience?
