# Genesis 42-50: Joseph Forgives His Brothers
## Sunbeams Lesson Plan (Ages 3-4)
*Prepared: 2026-03-22*

---

## The Big Story in One Sentence

Joseph's brothers were mean to him, but Joseph loved them anyway and forgave them — because that is what Heavenly Father wants us to do.

---

## Context (For the Teacher)

Genesis 42-50 is the conclusion of the Joseph story. After years as a slave and then a prisoner in Egypt, Joseph has risen to become second only to Pharaoh. When a famine strikes, his brothers travel to Egypt to buy grain — and unknowingly bow before the very brother they sold. Joseph recognizes them, tests them to see if their hearts have changed, and eventually reveals himself in one of the most emotionally charged scenes in all of scripture (Genesis 45:1-15). The brothers' fear of retaliation (Genesis 50:15-21) gives Joseph the opportunity to deliver the lesson's key verse: "Ye thought evil against me; but God meant it unto good" (Genesis 50:20).

Key verses for your own preparation:
- Genesis 45:4 — "I am Joseph your brother, whom ye sold into Egypt."
- Genesis 45:14-15 — Joseph fell on Benjamin's neck and wept; he kissed all his brothers.
- Genesis 50:17 — "Joseph wept when they spake unto him."
- Genesis 50:20-21 — "God meant it unto good... I will nourish you and your little ones."

## 5 Gospel Principles for Tiny Children

These are developmentally appropriate — concrete, physical, and tied to their world.

### 1. Heavenly Father Is Always Watching Over Us
Joseph went through very hard things (sold away from his family, put in prison), but Heavenly Father was with him the whole time. God told Jacob, "Fear not to go down into Egypt; for I will go down with thee" (Genesis 46:3). Heavenly Father is always with us too.
- **3-4 year old version:** "Even when things are hard, Heavenly Father loves us and is with us."

### 2. We Can Forgive People Who Hurt Us
Joseph's brothers were very mean to him. But when they came back, Joseph cried happy tears and hugged them. He said "I forgive you." That was very hard and very brave.
- **3-4 year old version:** "When someone is unkind, we can still choose to forgive them and love them."

### 3. Families Are Important
Joseph missed his family so much. When he saw his brother Benjamin, he had to run to his room to cry (Genesis 43:30). When he finally saw his father Jacob, "he fell on his neck, and wept on his neck a good while" (Genesis 46:29). Families are a gift from God.
- **3-4 year old version:** "Our family loves us, and we can show our family we love them."

### 4. We Can Be Kind Even When We Don't Have To Be
Joseph was a very powerful man in Egypt. He could have been mean back to his brothers. Instead, he filled their bags with food, gave them gifts, and told them "Fear not" (Genesis 50:21). We can always choose to be kind.
- **3-4 year old version:** "We can choose to be kind and share, even when we don't have to."

### 5. Heavenly Father Can Make Hard Things Turn Into Good Things
Joseph said, "Ye thought evil against me; but God meant it unto good" (Genesis 50:20). The hardest things in Joseph's life were the very things that let him save his whole family. Heavenly Father can turn our hard days into something good.
- **3-4 year old version:** "Even on hard days, Heavenly Father can help make things okay."

## Activity Ideas (4-5 Activities)

### Activity 1: FREEZE FRAME
**Theme:** Key moments in Joseph's story
**Time:** 8-10 minutes
**Materials:** None (or simple props: a cup, a small bag of "grain")

**How to play:**
Tell the children you are going to act out the story, and when the teacher says "FREEZE!" everyone freezes like a statue. Then the teacher asks a simple question about what just happened.

**Suggested scenes to act out:**

1. **The brothers bow down** — "Joseph was a very important man in Egypt. When his brothers came to get food, they bowed all the way down to the ground. Let's bow down... FREEZE! Who did they bow down to? (Joseph!)"

2. **Joseph cries happy tears** — "When Joseph saw his brother Benjamin, he was SO happy he started to cry. Show me your happy-cry face... FREEZE! Why was Joseph crying? (Because he was SO happy to see his brother!)"

3. **The big hug** — "Joseph fell on his brother Benjamin's neck and hugged him tight and cried. Everybody hug your neighbor tight... FREEZE! Joseph forgave his brothers. That means he wasn't mad anymore. Can we forgive people?"

4. **Jacob and Joseph reunite** — "Joseph's dad Jacob thought Joseph was dead. But Joseph was ALIVE! When Jacob saw Joseph he hugged him and cried for a long, long time. Show me the biggest hug you can give... FREEZE! How does it feel to hug someone you love?"

5. **Fear not** — "After their dad died, the brothers were scared Joseph would be mad. But Joseph said 'Fear not! I will take care of you and your children.' Show me a scared face... now show me a happy face... FREEZE! Joseph was kind even though he didn't have to be."

**Teacher tip:** Keep each scene very short (30 seconds). Ham it up! The sillier and more dramatic you are, the more the children will delight in it.

---

### Activity 2: Fill the Sack
**Theme:** Joseph filled his brothers' sacks with grain
**Time:** 5 minutes
**Materials:** Small cloth bags or paper lunch bags, crumpled paper balls or dried beans

Joseph filled his brothers' bags with food to take home, even though they had been mean to him. That was kind!

Let each child fill a little bag with "grain" (crumpled paper balls, dried beans in a sealed container, or even goldfish crackers). Ask: "Joseph filled the bags even though his brothers had been unkind. What does that tell us about Joseph? What could we fill up for someone we love?"

**Optional:** Let them take the bag home as a reminder to be kind.

---

### Activity 3: Forgiveness Circle
**Theme:** Forgiving someone who was unkind
**Time:** 5 minutes
**Materials:** None

Sit in a circle. Act out a simple scenario:
- The teacher "bumps" a child's arm (very gently, playfully).
- The teacher says: "Oops! I'm sorry."
- The child says: "I forgive you."
- Then do a big hug.

Let the kids take turns being the one who forgives. Talk about it:
- "Was it easy to say 'I forgive you'?"
- "How did Joseph feel when he forgave his brothers?"
- "Heavenly Father is SO happy when we forgive each other."

Keep it light and giggly — at this age, the physical act of the hug is the lesson.

---

### Activity 4: Joseph's Journey Counting Game
**Theme:** God was with Joseph through many hard things
**Time:** 5 minutes
**Materials:** Drawn or printed pictures, or use fingers

Count out the hard things that happened to Joseph on your fingers:
1. His brothers were mean to him.
2. He had to go far away from his family.
3. He was put in jail.
4. He had to wait a long, long time.

Then flip each finger back down counting the good things:
1. He made a friend in jail.
2. He got to help a king.
3. His brothers came back.
4. He saw his dad again!
5. He saved his whole family!

"Heavenly Father was with Joseph the whole time. Heavenly Father is with us too!"

---

### Activity 5: Happy Tears, Sad Tears Faces
**Theme:** Big feelings are okay; we can feel them and still choose kindness
**Time:** 3-4 minutes
**Materials:** Paper plates with simple faces drawn on them (optional)

Joseph cried a LOT in these chapters — but they were often happy tears mixed with sad tears. Name some feelings and let the kids make the face:
- "Show me a SAD face — Joseph missed his family."
- "Show me a HAPPY face — Joseph saw Benjamin!"
- "Show me a SURPRISED face — the brothers were so surprised Joseph was alive!"
- "Show me a BRAVE face — Joseph was brave enough to forgive."
- "Show me a LOVING face — Joseph said 'I will take care of you.'"

End by asking: "Which face do you want to show at home today?"

## Suggested Songs from the Children's Songbook

### Primary Picks

1. **"Families Can Be Together Forever"** (CSB p. 188)
   - Direct connection: Jacob thought he had lost Joseph forever. Families being together is one of the greatest joys in this story. Jacob's words, "Joseph my son is yet alive" (Genesis 45:28), echo this truth.
   - Great for: Opening or closing the lesson.

2. **"I'm Trying to Be Like Jesus"** (CSB p. 78)
   - Direct connection: Joseph showed Christlike love in forgiving his brothers, feeding them, and saying "Fear not."
   - Use the phrase: "Love one another as Jesus loves you. Try to show kindness in all that you do."

3. **"A Child's Prayer"** (CSB p. 12)
   - Direct connection: God spoke to Jacob in a vision (Genesis 46:2-4) and reassured him. Heavenly Father hears and answers our prayers.
   - Good for: Quiet, reverent moment in the lesson.

4. **"Love One Another"** (CSB p. 136)
   - Direct connection: Joseph's forgiveness is one of the greatest examples of "Love one another as I have loved you" in all scripture.
   - Short, singable, and perfectly on-theme.

5. **"He Sent His Son"** (CSB p. 34)
   - Direct connection: Joseph as a type of Christ — sent by his father, rejected by his brothers, yet the means of saving his family's lives. You don't need to teach typology to Sunbeams, but this song plants the seed: Heavenly Father loves us so much He gave us someone to save us.
   - Use for: Closing thought or simple testimony.

## Simple Story Script for 3-4 Year Olds

*Read slowly, with big expressions:*

"Joseph had a big family — lots and lots of brothers! But his brothers were not very nice to him. They did something very mean. Joseph had to go far, far away from his family.

Joseph was sad. He missed his dad. He missed his brothers. But Heavenly Father was with Joseph. And Joseph worked hard and became a very important helper to the king of Egypt.

One day, Joseph's brothers came to Egypt because they were very hungry. And guess what? They didn't know it was Joseph! But Joseph knew it was THEM.

[Big eyes] Joseph had to try SO hard not to cry. He went to his room and he cried and cried.

And then — one day — he told them the truth. He said: 'I AM JOSEPH! I am your brother!'

[Surprised face] His brothers were SO surprised! And scared! They thought Joseph would be angry.

But Joseph said — can you guess? — He said: 'Don't be scared. I forgive you. Heavenly Father made everything okay. I love you.'

And then Joseph gave his brothers a HUGE hug. [Hug yourself or the child next to you.]

And Joseph brought his dad all the way to Egypt. And when Jacob — that's his dad — when Jacob saw Joseph, he hugged him and cried for a looooong time. Happy, happy tears.

Joseph taught us something very important: we can forgive people, even when they were really mean to us. That's what Heavenly Father wants us to do. And that's what Jesus did too."

## Teacher Notes

- The story has heavy content (slavery, brothers betraying a brother) — none of that needs to be named with Sunbeams. Focus entirely on: Joseph was away from his family, Joseph forgave, Joseph was kind, Joseph hugged.
- Joseph weeping is a beautiful, child-accessible emotional moment. Don't skip it — children understand tears, and knowing it's okay for grownups to cry happy tears is healthy.
- Genesis 50:21 is the most child-ready verse: "I will nourish you, and your little ones." Joseph taking care of the little ones is something a 3-4 year old can feel in their bones.
- The FREEZE FRAME activity works best if the teacher is enthusiastic and theatrical. The goal is giggling and moving, not sitting still.
